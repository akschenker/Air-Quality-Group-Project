Project Motivation

During the recent Camp Fire that proceeded to become the most destructive wildfire in California history - and which significantly affected air quality for hundreds of miles - air quality was on the forefront of our minds. We sought to analyze various information about the air quality in cities around the world. Our data was obtained from Breezometer, an API for extracting air quality information based on various parameters. One of the reasons we selected BreezoMeter was due to its standardized air quality index. An air quality index (AQI) is a number used to communicate how polluted the air currently is. Today, there is no globally standardized approach to providing air quality information, meaning AQIs differ from country to country. BreezoMeter has created a new uniform index: the BreezoMeter Air Quality Index (BAQI). BAQI values range between 100 (Excellent air quality) and 0 (Poor air quality), and is determined based on concentrations of predefined air pollutants.



Data Overview

We obtained a list of the 500 most populous cities from the MaxMind Database. Using latitudes and longitudes, we were able to obtain air quality data for 398 cities using the Python requests library. This allowed us to create custom URLs, based on the latitude and longitude of each city, to request data from Breezometer, and receive it in a JSON format. We requested data about air quality, concentration of pollutants, and recommendations for citizens in different segments of the population (e.g., general, children, elderly).

Extracting and Sorting Data from Breezometer

After obtaining data from Breezometer in JSON format, we used a Python script to write the data to a local instance of MongoDB. We accomplished this by indexing into the JSON object to obtain the aspects of the data that we wanted, then assigning those to documents in MongoDB.

Visualizing Data using HTML/CSS/JavaScript

To display the data on a webpage, we used the Flask Python Microframework and the Flask-PyMongo library to obtain data from our local instance of MongoDB and send it to the front end to be rendered on our HTML page. Specifically, we sent the data to JavaScript and were able to use d3 to render it on the webpage.

Making the Data Accessible Remotely

We were able to deploy our webpages to Heroku in order for it to be accessed more easily. Heroku is a server that allows us to store our data via a remote link, installs all our dependencies on that server, and renders the webpage for any user that visits without requiring them to have dependencies installed.



Results and Analysis

Our findings indicate that the air quality in densely populated cities tend to be worse on the Indian sub-continent and along east-Asia, and slightly better in the Western Hemisphere. However, this data is not necessarily reflective of the air quality of large regions of land since the data we analyzed only consisted of cities with populations of more than half a million people.

We noticed air quality and city population did not correlate, as Tokyo, the most populous city in our database, had "good" air quality, despite having a population of over 30 million people.
